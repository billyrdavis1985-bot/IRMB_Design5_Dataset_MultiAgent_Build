## Abstract

This repository documents a novel dataset construction methodology for frontier
scientific claim evaluation. The dataset supports IRMB Phase 7G Design 5 —
a pre-registered quantum-AI coordination study testing whether real quantum
processing unit measurement distributions produce statistically superior
LLM council coordination compared to classical and matched-distribution baselines.

The methodology introduces a **Multi-Agent Adversarial Council** approach to
dataset generation: six frontier AI models independently generate candidate
articles, each scored on eight scientific dimensions by blind cross-validation.
The highest-scoring council member becomes lead generator for the final dataset.
Every article requires named researcher disputes, real verifiable DOIs,
and passes dual statistical validation gates before acceptance.

**No comparable public dataset exists** at this ambiguity threshold,
specificity level, and methodological rigor for frontier scientific claims
across quantum computing, AI capability, frontier physics, and biotech/longevity.

## The Multi-Agent Adversarial Council Methodology

### What Makes This Novel

Existing scientific claim datasets are typically generated by:
- Single research teams with potential domain bias
- Automated scraping with limited quality control
- Human annotators without adversarial validation

This work introduces a fundamentally different approach.

### Council Architecture

Six frontier AI models were independently prompted with identical generation
instructions, then evaluated and scored before any articles were accepted:

| Model | Role | Score | Strength |
|-------|------|-------|----------|
| Claude | Lead generator (final 60%) | 98/100 | Zero fabricated sources, named researcher rationale, perfect balance |
| Gemini | Validator + co-generator | 86/100 | Best rationale quality, named institutional disputes |
| Grok | Co-generator | 76/100 | Strong R1 batch, high volume delivery |
| GPT-4o | Co-generator | 72/100 | Clean DOIs, strong AI capability articles |
| DeepSeek | Co-generator | 64/100 | Excellent AGI timeline articles, balance issues |
| Perplexity | Methodology consultant | DNF | Produced framework not executable JSON |
| NeMo (NVIDIA) | Test evaluation | 25/100 | Confirmed as analysis tool not generator |

### Scoring Rubric (8 dimensions, 100 points total)

| Dimension | Points | Criteria |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Volume compliance | 10 | Delivered requested article count |
| Balance accuracy | 15 | 50/50 true/false per batch |
| Source quality | 20 | Real verifiable DOIs, no fabrication |
| Ambiguity quality | 15 | Genuine >= 0.75 scores |
| Claim quality | 15 | Plausible overstatements, not trivially false |
| Rationale quality | 10 | Named researchers on both sides |
| Novelty/coverage | 10 | New territory, no duplicates |
| Format compliance | 5 | Correct IDs, categories, schema |

### Article Quality Standard

Every accepted article must satisfy all of the following:

```
MANDATORY:
  ✅ Real published paper source (arXiv, Nature, NEJM, PRL, etc.)
  ✅ Published within last 24 months
  ✅ Binary ground truth (true or false)
  ✅ Ambiguity score >= 0.75
     (3 PhD experts would genuinely disagree)
  ✅ Difficulty: hard or extreme only
  ✅ Named researchers on BOTH sides of the dispute
  ✅ Specific institutional or methodological disagreement
  ✅ Claim is a plausible overstatement of real findings
     NOT: trivially false absolute language
     YES: specific boundary condition disputes

PROHIBITED:
  ❌ Fabricated or placeholder DOIs
  ❌ "to_be_verified" sources
  ❌ "ALL", "EVERY", "ZERO", "COMPLETE", "IDENTICAL" framing
  ❌ Generic "experts disagree" rationale without names
  ❌ Claims answerable by simple web search
  ❌ Ground truth below ambiguity threshold 0.75
```

## Validation Architecture

### Dual Validation Gate (C5 Sampler)

The matched classical distribution sampler (C5_Matched) for the quantum
coordination experiment requires passing both gates before execution:

```
Gate 1: KL(sampled || ibm_fez source) < 0.01
Gate 2: chi2 test non-significant (p > 0.05)

Both must pass. If either fails: resample and refit.
Retries: up to 5 attempts per angle.
```

Validation results from build session:

| Angle | KL | chi2 | p-value | Status |
|-------|----|------|---------|--------|
| AB | 0.001509 | 6.2127 | 0.1017 | ✅ PASS |
| ABp | 0.001015 | 4.0057 | 0.2609 | ✅ PASS |
| ApB | 0.000237 | 0.9623 | 0.8104 | ✅ PASS |
| ApBp | 0.000446 | 1.7851 | 0.6182 | ✅ PASS |

### Dataset Validation Pipeline

```
Cell 1:  Setup + schema definition

Cell 3:  Gemini + DeepSeek clean articles
```

## Honest Limitations

```
1. Articles are generated by AI models and require independent
   verification of source DOIs before use in downstream research.

2. Ground truth labels for frontier claims involve genuine uncertainty.
   The binary label reflects best expert judgment at time of generation.

3. Named researcher attributions are drawn from published literature
   and may not reflect current positions of those researchers.

4. The dataset is specifically designed for the IRMB Design 5 protocol.
   Generalization to other evaluation frameworks requires validation.
```
